(5) Thomas Wolcott Jr., b. 1726 Tolland CT, d. c.1792 Amenia NY. In 1759-1760, Thomas Wolcott was elected "pender" of Crum Elbow Precinct, which included Northeast and Amenia townships. In 1760 he purchased property on Wetbutuck Creek in Amenia Township, including a sawmill and dam, and privilege of a grist mill, in 1762 he was appointed Overseer of Highways at the first town meeting of Amenia; his widow and son, Luke, sold the land at Amenia with dwelling and blacksmith shop in 1774; the blacksmith shop was still standing in 1996. Thomas m. Catherine Sackett, sometimes said to be daughter of Richard Sackett. This is in error as this Catherine was b. 1702, 24 years before Thomas, and her youngest child would have been born at age 60. Richard Sackett's will dated 1744 and proved 1746 leaves a bequest to his daughter, Catherine, "during her widowhood". In a court case regarding her father's will she is called Catherine Margeson, which was apparently her married name. Thomas' wife was probably Catherine, b. c.1720, daughter of Catherine's brother, John Sackett.

He was married in 1836 to Miss Elizabeth Cruson, one of twelve sisters, his present widow, and soon after removed to Erie Co. Ohio, where he continued to reside until six years ago when he removed to this place. Since then he has made this his home. During his early wedded life he was sorely pushed at times to get the necessities of life, but by industry and frugality he succeeded in amassing a competency sufficient to leave his widow and daughter above want. He was always generous to the poor, helping those who needed help, and never distressing those who had business relations with him, as many can testify. During his longand painful illness not one impatient word passed his lips. "It is all right", was his frequent expression, willing to live for the sake of his blind wife and saddened household of his only child, yet prepared to die, he quietly passed away on the 13th of February 1884 at the age of 71 years and 3 months, leaving a host of friends and relatives to mourn his death."
